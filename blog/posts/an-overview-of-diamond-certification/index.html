An Overview of Diamond Certification

January 15, 2024 - 7 min read

Certified diamonds have undergone a grading process at a gem laboratory of high repute. Their grade is determined by their “4 C” qualities. The aforementioned qualities consist of cut, color, clarity, and carat weight. Purchasing certified diamonds is consistently advised to guarantee the acquisition of a stone of superior quality. Every certified diamond is accompanied by a diamond certification, which is an elaborate document that delineates the stone’s most critical characteristics. A certified diamond’s quality is consistently verified by a third party. This verification is performed by gemologists who are qualified to designate precise grades of diamonds and are well-versed in their inner workings.

An Analysis of a Diamond Certificate

There are several aspects to scrutinize when examining the GIA certificate of a diamond:

  • The date of grading the diamond
  • The report number of the GIA
  • Form and method of cutting (e.g., round dazzling)
  • Measurements

The certificate will then include the grading outcomes for the carat weight, cut grade, clarity grade, and color grade. Further criteria for determining quality are polish, symmetry, and fluorescence. In addition, the certificate provides an in-depth view of the diamond, detailing its precise dimensions and clarity attributes. To observe an illustrative instance of a diamond grading report, please visit the GIA’s website.

Different Types of Diamond Grading System 

In addition to the GIA grading system, which is widely recognized and regarded as one of the most reliable, other diamond certification include: AGS, IGI, EGL, GSI, and HRD.

GIA Certification

 The Gemological Institute of America (GIA) is the preeminent and widely recognized organization for grading diamonds. They offer unparalleled assurance and remarkable consistency when it comes to the acquisition of any diamond.  Assessing diamonds on a multitude of qualities and elements, the GIA has no monetary interest in the diamond’s sale.

GIA is renowned for assigning more rigorous ratings to the most intuitive scales, namely Color and Clarity. American Gem Society (AGS)

A collective of jewelers established the American Gem Society in 1934 with the primary objective of safeguarding the public who purchases jewelry against deceit and fraudulent advertising. On a scale of zero to ten, they assess carat, clarity, cut, and color; zero represents the finest quality, and ten represents the lowest. AGS is currently comprised of three thousand members, including retailers, suppliers, and jewelers, and is regarded as a dependable certification authority for diamonds.

International Gemological Institute (IGI)

Since 1975, the International Gemological Institute has maintained laboratory facilities in eighteen different countries. IGI assessed both loose diamonds and completed jewelry. Commencing in 2005, it was the inaugural gemological institute to conduct comprehensive grading of laboratory-grown diamonds.

European Gemological Laboratories (EGL)

Typical components of reports issued by European Gemological Laboratories comprise the following: carat weight, clarity grade, color grade, cut (including shape and style), finish, fluorescence, proportions, and plotting. Furthermore, they are frequently criticized for having more lax grading criteria. 

Gemological Science International (GSI)  

Since its inception in 2005, Gemological Science International has graded lab-grown and naturally occurring diamonds. The laboratories utilize state-of-the-art technology and instruments to quantify the light performance of diamonds. The resulting reports encompass comprehensive details such as weight, shape, color, transparency, fluorescence, polish, uniformity, evaluations, cut grade, and a plotting diagram.

Hoge Raad voor Diamant (HRD) 

Specializing in the issuance of certificates for gemstones and diamonds, Hoge Raad voor Diamant is headquartered in Antwerp, Belgium, a region renowned for its diamond industry. The organization employs a “double coding system” to generate reports pertaining to laboratory-grown, treated, and natural diamonds.

Importance of Diamond Certification

Diamond certification proves it’s natural

Jewelers and appraisers lack the extensive testing equipment available to gem laboratories. For instance, although the majority of appraisers possess standard equipment, they don’t typically have a method for testing lab-grown diamonds or diamond procedures. Certification of a diamond thus verifies that the gemstone you are purchasing is natural and not synthetic.

Certification Signifies Diamonds Quality

A diamond certification is essential because it represents an objective assessment of the stone’s quality. The degree of variation in grading among jewelers and appraisers is substantial. When contrasting this with gem laboratories, diamond grading methods are consistent. Put simply, the reports produced by jewelers and appraisers lack the reliability and precision associated with certifications.

As we shall elaborate further below, certain gem laboratories adhere to these guidelines more stringently than others. The most reliable certifications, which we refer to as “credible” diamond certifications, characterize the quality of the diamond in an honest and precise manner. A reliable diamond certification is therefore the most effective method to ensure that one receives value for their investment.
