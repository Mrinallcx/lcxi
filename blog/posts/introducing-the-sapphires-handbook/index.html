The handbook includes five foundational articles exploring key gemological factors that influence a sapphire’s beauty, grading, and market value. Whether you’re new to sapphires or seeking deeper knowledge before investing, these readings offer clarity, depth, and practical understanding.

🔍 What You’ll Learn in the Sapphires Handbook

1. Understanding Sapphires: Origin, Color, and Market Position

This article breaks down the historical and commercial importance of sapphires, with special attention to Sri Lanka (Ceylon) — one of the oldest and most respected sources of high-quality stones. It explains how sapphires from different regions exhibit unique color signatures, clarity levels, and investment value.

2. The Art and Science of Sapphire Cutting

Learn how the cut of a gemstone impacts light return, color saturation, and value. From table size and symmetry to crown height and pavilion depth, cutting decisions are often a balance between preserving carat weight and optimizing brilliance.

3. Brightness, Darkness & Brilliance: What Makes a Sapphire Sparkle

Explore how light behaves inside a sapphire, and how factors like extinction, windowing, and observer reflection shape the way a gem looks face-up. This section also explains the contrast pattern — a crucial element in how “lively” or “flat” a gem appears.

4. Craftsmanship and Evaluation Standards

What separates an average sapphire from an exceptional one often comes down to craftsmanship. This article outlines how experts assess symmetry, polish, facet alignment, girdle quality, and overall design — and how each of these factors plays into value perception.

5. Large Colored Stones and Their Unique Pricing Dynamics

Larger sapphires don’t just scale in value by weight — they often appreciate exponentially due to rarity. This article covers how size, proportion, and color consistency in larger stones influence both retail and investment markets.

🧪 Certified Quality from Trusted Gemological Laboratories


All sapphires offered on Tiamonds are certified by leading gemstone laboratories such as GRS, CGL, and others, ensuring authenticity, origin verification, and clarity on any treatments or enhancements. These certifications are a cornerstone of transparency and trust in both the gem and tokenized asset markets.
