How to Take Care of Diamonds?

February 8, 2024 - 5 min read

Despite being among the hardest gems on the planet and potentially as ancient as the earth itself,. Diamonds still require cherishing and maintenance to preserve their brilliance. While these are the hardest substances found in nature, the care of diamonds is extremely important.

 As with all other substances, diamonds can eventually lose their brilliance. Diamonds, despite being recognized as the hardest substance to date, can still experience edge chipping. If not taken care of, diamonds could even fall from their setting, rendering an ornament essentially useless. However, diamond pendants, bangles, earrings, and rings can retain their brand-new appearance for years with proper maintenance.

Regular Cleaning of Diamond Jewelry 

A diamond emits brilliance due to the manner in which light traverses it. However, debris, oil, and perspiration can obstruct the transmission of light. Thus, you cannot expect your diamonds to sparkle optimally if you do not routinely clean them. The care of diamonds requires little effort to polish them. Warm water, a gentle toothbrush, and mild detergent are all that are required. Do not use toothpaste to clean gemstones. After a few minutes of soaking in soapy water, delicately cleanse your diamonds with a soft-bristled toothbrush.

Additionally, clear the back of the diamond, where dirt accumulates readily, as well as its face. Dry the surface using a gentle, lint-free cloth. Before cleaning your diamond jewelry in the sink or washbasin, make sure the receptacle is completely clogged. A cleaning of diamonds should ideally occur every two weeks. Using chemicals to clean gemstones should be avoided to prevent erosion or dissolution of the metal prongs. This may cause the metal prongs to erode or even dissolve.

Touch Them Gently

The prongs that secure diamonds can break, even though diamonds themselves are difficult to shatter. Therefore, avoid handling your diamond ornaments roughly. Do not scrub them excessively vigorously or apply excessive pressure when washing them. Take care of  diamonds by avoiding cleaning with a rigid toothbrush for the same reason. Avoid removing a diamond from your garments if it becomes entangled. Remove it gently from the fabric. You really do not want to spend an entire day looking for a small diamond on the floor.

Maintenance of a Diamond Ring

In the context of removing rings, it is imperative to consistently grasp the item by its band rather than by its stone or setting. This will aid in maintaining the diamond’s position in its setting and prevent the accumulation of natural oils from your palms around the stone.

At-home diamond ring cleaning is as straightforward as applying boiling soapy water and using a soft-bristled toothbrush.

Rinse thoroughly with a solution of one part ammonia to six parts water in order to restore the luster.

Non-abrasive jewelry cleansers available for purchase are a suitable substitute for brushing. Ensure that the cleaner is suitable for use on both the diamond and the ring metal. To take proper care of diamonds, rub them delicately with a soft cloth after cleansing to enhance their luster.

Every six months, it is advisable to have an authorized jeweler professionally clean your diamond. Additionally, the jeweler can examine your ring for any signs of damage or fragility, ensuring that the diamond remains secure in its setting.

Constantly wearing diamonds is not their intended design.

Understanding when to wear diamonds and when to store them in a jewelry case is crucial. Diamonds can be worn frequently, but they may not be suitable for every occasion. If you are planning a beach vacation, for instance, it would be prudent to leave your diamond bracelets and earrings at home. This is particularly accurate regarding diamond jewelry. Sand and salt can not only contaminate diamonds but also cause finger atrophy and facilitate the detachment of jewelry.

Before you start cleaning with bleach or chlorine, make sure to remove your diamonds. Considering how your diamonds are set can help you decide whether to keep them or leave them at home. Due to the susceptibility of jewelry containing micropave stones to damage, it is unwise to do so while engaging in vigorous activities. Furthermore, it is unadvisable to wear diamonds to the gym or during exercise.

Keep Them  Carefully

Furthermore, diamonds must be protected from injury in addition to loss and theft. When rubbed together, diamonds can cause scratches on each other. Additionally, diamonds have the potential to cause damage and scratching to any other jewelry they come into contact with. Diamonds cannot regain their original brilliance once they sustain an injury. Therefore, it is advisable to store every piece of jewelry separately and prevent them from coming into contact with one another. Investing in a fabric-lined jewelry case featuring individual compartments to accommodate all of your diamond jewelry is a prudent decision. An alternative method would be to encase every individual diamond jewelry item in delicate tissue.

Diamonds Require Routine Examination

In addition to the stones themselves, the care of diamonds requires safety for the metal frameworks that secure them in place. Verify that every prong supporting your diamond is of equivalent length. It is necessary to have a jeweler evaluate your jewelry when one becomes shorter than the others. A jeweler may also utilize an ultrasonic cleanser to assist in the lustering of your diamonds. This process cleans the filth and grime that have accumulated on the metal and diamonds using a vibrating fluid. Concerning diamond-encrusted rings, ensure that the ring fits properly. If the ring is loosened, it will subject the diamond to greater degradation.

However, you can only preserve the attractiveness of diamond jewelry with additional care. By following these guidelines, you can ensure that your diamonds remain flawless and as beautiful as they were when you first put them on.
