Challenges in the Diamond Industry 

Ethical Sourcing of Diamonds

The infamous concern is the trade in “conflict diamonds” or “blood diamonds,” extracted in areas affected by armed conflict and sold to fund armed resistance against governments. These practices give rise to significant ethical considerations.

The tokenization of diamonds and Tiamonds resolves this issue in two ways: traceability and certification, and permanent records.

Traceability and Certification: The process of tokenization offers a dependable means of monitoring the whereabouts and development of every tokenized diamond. The process of tracing the movement of a diamond from the mine to the vault and facilitates the verification of its non-conflict status.

Tiamonds maintain an immutable ledger where information regarding the conflict-free status of a diamond is recorded. Once appended, this data remains unmodifiable, thereby enhancing the credibility of ethical claims.


Lack of Transparency

Frequently, the opaque nature of the diamond supply chain makes tracing the origin of a diamond challenging. This lack of transparency could lead to consumers buying illegal or unethical diamonds.

The tokenization of diamonds on tiamonds addresses this issue in two ways: a transparent supply chain and universal access to information.

Tiamonds maintains a transparent supply chain that allows purchasers to authenticate the diamond’s provenance and historical background.

The provision of information by Tiamonds enables the general public to access prestige assets, enabling consumers to authenticate the origin and trajectory of the diamonds they have acquired.

Model of Direct Ownership

In the direct ownership paradigm, each token represents a fraction of the underlying asset. Consequently, token holders acquire exclusive ownership privileges in addition to a proportionate share of the asset’s value, financial flows, and any additional benefits associated with it. 

The following are some critical factors to keep in mind:

Facilitated Accessibility

Investors of all sizes can now participate in an asset class that was previously restricted to wealthy individuals or institutional entities through direct ownership of tokenized natural resources.

Enhanced Liquidity

The division of fractional shares into tokens facilitates the buying and selling of the asset on digital platforms. As a result, investors benefit from enhanced market accessibility and increased liquidity.

Opportunities for Diversification of Portfolios

Direct ownership investments provide investors with exposure to a diverse range of natural resource categories without necessitating the purchase of entire properties.

Model of Indirect Ownership

The indirect ownership model entails the tokenization of shares in corporations that possess or administer natural resource assets, as opposed to the direct ownership approach. As opposed to direct ownership rights, token holders possess shares in a corporation that owns the underlying assets. 

The following information pertains to this model:

Investors can attain exposure to a variety of natural resource assets through indirect ownership, which entails possessing shares in companies operating within the sector.

Investing in corporations via tokens grants one entry to expert management teams tasked with the oversight of daily operations and the formulation of strategic decisions.

Regulatory Considerations: The utilization of tokens as representations of company shares necessitates adherence to regulatory frameworks that regulate investments and securities. This results in increased compliance requirements and complexity for issuers and investors alike.

Blockchain Technology Analysis in Tokenization

Blockchain functions as the foundational technology that facilitates the generation of digital tokens that symbolize ownership or entitlements to natural resources. By virtue of its decentralized and immutable characteristics. Blockchain technology facilitates the smooth transfer of asset ownership and maintains an audit trail of transactions that is unlikely to alter.

Advantages of Traceability and Transparency

An important benefit of utilizing blockchain technology for the tokenization of natural resources is the increased level of transparency it provides. A distributed ledger logs each transaction and transfer of ownership. Thereby furnishing stakeholders with instantaneous visibility into the whereabouts of assets and alterations in ownership. An elevated degree of transparency cultivates confidence among participants and mitigates the potential for fraudulent activities.

Smart Contract Advantages

Smart contracts enhance the efficiency of the tokenization procedure by executing themselves and containing predefined terms written in code. These contracts have the capability to automate a multitude of facets pertaining to natural resources, including but not limited to revenue allocation, royalties, and adherence to regulatory obligations. This automation not only reduces administrative costs but also ensures precise fulfilment of contractual obligations.
