Top Tokenization Use Cases

#digitalassets #realworldassets #tokenization
May 16, 2024 - 6 min read

The financial sector widely recognizes tokenization as a cutting-edge innovation that applies the advantageous features of blockchain technology to conventional assets. Tokenization enables the conversion of assets into digital tokens, which facilitate their transmission, management, and trading via Distributed Ledger Technology. In addition to commodities, tokenization use cases from real estate, stocks, and bonds, these tokens may also represent art and mementos. As tokenization disrupts a wide variety of industries and markets, numerous use cases can benefit substantially. Not only does tokenization lead to significant enhancements in overall process efficiency. But it also offers the chance to earn extra revenue from assets previously not included in the investment scope.

The Rise of Token Development

As the prevalence of cryptocurrencies increased, so did the need for token development. Within distinct blockchain ecosystems, tokens serve as digital assets capable of utilization, storage, and exchange. In addition to facilitating transactions and accessing services, they may also be used to represent proprietorship of tangible assets. The profusion of opportunities sparked by the adaptability and versatility of tokens gave birth to Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs) and the token economy as a whole.


The Story of Tokenization of Real-World Assets

Since the real estate industry first considered the potential of tokenizing real and virtual assets. Progressive industrialists have been intrigued by the prospect of tokens representing additional assets. This vision became a reality with the advent of tokenization for tangible assets, giving rise to a thriving market currently valued at billions of dollars.

RWA tokenization has emerged as a transformative factor in propelling the cryptocurrency economy beyond its adverse phase in 2023. It implements the potential advantages that blockchains can extend to the tangible realm. Presently, the blockchain enables multiple tokenization use cases for virtually any tangible object and the distribution of applicable rights on a global scale.

Under ideal market conditions, the market for real-world asset tokens could soar to $10 trillion by 2030, according to a report. Such indications have bolstered the viability of a variety of RWA use cases that we will observe in the future. It suggests that operating a company based on the model will be an excellent endeavour.

Furthermore, the integration of tokens into tangible assets will improve the operational strategies of corporations in the future, when community engagement will be a critical determinant. Large corporations across industries may adopt RWA tokenization to varying degrees. As its comprehensive implementation could potentially impact their overall profits.

Innovative Tokenization Use Cases

Equipments

Tokenization of tangible assets in the machinery industry is an innovative use case that has had a significant impact on the market recently. It enables the tokenization of expensive, large-scale machinery. This is not only facilitates investment but also offers more consistent passive income streams. This application scenario is essential for DePINs to accomplish their intended purpose. 

Private Data

Although the topic of tokenizing personal data has been under discussion for quite some time. The recent surge in RWA tokenization has effectively sparked a flood of activity. Individuals may tokenize and sell personally identifiable information they generate online to interested parties with analytic intentions. This feature enhances data privacy by giving users authority over the use of their data.

Mineral Assets

The process of tokenizing energy assets has emerged as a significant catalyst within the energy sector. Particularly in light of the global competition towards implementing sustainable energy production techniques. On-chain tokenization of carbon credits, wind farms, solar farms, and biogas facilities grants token holders lucrative revenue streams and decision-making authority.

Consumer Products

Consumer goods tokenization has emerged as a critical use case for tokenizing RWAs, despite the fact that it is uncommon. Users can acquire tokens representing foods, beverages, pharmaceuticals, and apparel through membership programs and investment initiatives. These securities may facilitate the initial public offering of consumer-focused companies without requiring intermediaries.

Agricultural Products

The tokenization of agricultural products serves as the catalyst for increased investment in the farming sector. Tokenization of components such as land, commodities, equipment, and processing and storage facilities on the blockchain. It enables retail investors and agriculture enthusiasts to participate in a sector that is vital to the global economy.

Luxurious Items

The luxury sector has experienced a notable upsurge in tokenization due to the entry of numerous renowned brands and enterprises in the industry into the blockchain domain. For businesses operating in the fashion, footwear, wearables, and travel industries. The issuance of tokens that symbolize digital iterations of their products can help them regain their products’ authenticity and market appeal.

Already-Established RWA Tokenization Use Cases

Physical Collectibles

Several types of physical collectibles have undergone tokenization on-chain in recent years, rendering expensive assets more liquid. Tokens can represent otherwise difficult-to-obtain objects like fine art, classic vehicles, antique collectibles, and technological products, facilitating their sale.

Financial Assets

The significant adoption of financial instrument tokenization as a practical application is evidenced by the 600% increase in demand for tokens representing U.S. Treasury bonds in 2023. To facilitate capital flow, securities, equity shares, debt bonds, and proofs of deposit may be tokenized and sold to retail investors.

Commodities

Tokenized RWAs are increasingly being utilized for commodity tokenization as an increasing number of ventures contemplate going public via blockchains. Thus improving their appeal in a market that is becoming more digitally savvy. Tokenization of tangible assets such as real estate, gold, silver, diamonds, and other rare metals can appeal to investors of varying sizes and scopes across the globe.

Intellectual Property

Regarding ownership and utilization rights of data, intellectual property (IP) has historically been a contentious topic. The process of tokenizing patents, copyrights, trademarks, and licenses may be of utmost importance in addressing these concerns, as concerned parties can acquire tokens containing utility and rights via on-chain transactions.

Funds of Venture Capital (VC)

Tokenizing venture capital (VC) funds, which are increasingly becoming an integral component of the business investment framework, can result in a number of substantial advantages that reshape the investment flow. In the cash-rich domain of venture capital funding, tokens representing these funds may grant fractional ownership, governance rights, company shares, and necessary investigation capabilities.

Royalties

Since blockchain technology invaded the music industry, royalties have acquired new meanings. At this time, event tickets, music albums, fan merchandise, royalty shares, and merchandise can be tokenized, bridging the gap between artists and diehard supporters. This application of RWA tokenization improves the prospects for individuals aspiring to pursue a music-related career.

To Conclude
