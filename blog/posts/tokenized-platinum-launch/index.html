1. Introduction to Platinum

Platinum stands as a powerful asset, scarcer than gold and indispensable to industries such as automotive, clean energy, and medical technologies. It is admired not only for its industrial utility but also as a store of wealth.

Platinum’s role in high-value applications and its increasing relevance in green technologies make it an asset with both financial and societal impact. Now, with Tiamonds, investing in this asset is easier and more transparent than ever before.

2. Market Insight and Pricing Trends

Platinum Price Update

Platinum recently extended its rally to a sixth consecutive session, reaching $1,200.95 per ounce, its highest level since May 2021. This 32% year-to-date increase has even outpaced gold’s 26% rise.

What’s Driving the Price Surge?

  • Tight Supply Expectations: Concerns about limited output have pushed prices upward.
  • Industrial Sentiment Recovery: Stronger demand across automotive and green tech sectors is boosting investor confidence.
  • Technical Momentum: Platinum is riding the broader rally in precious metals.

“There’s been growing tightness since December,” said Ed Sterck, Director of Research at the World Platinum Investment Council (WPIC).

In addition:

  • Lease rates in London and Zurich vaults have surged to record highs – now above 13.5% annually, showing extreme market tightness.
  • ETF holdings in platinum have reached a 10-month high.
  • Spot prices are trading above futures, another strong bullish indicator.

WPIC projects a near 1 million ounce supply deficit in 2025, underscoring the metal’s growing scarcity.

3. Why Invest in Platinum?

Store of Value

Platinum serves as a tangible hedge against inflation and the depreciation of fiat currencies. It offers portfolio protection, especially during economic uncertainty.

Global Recognition

Held by central banks and financial institutions, platinum is globally recognized for both its economic and industrial worth.

Hedge Against Inflation

Due to its rarity and essential use cases, platinum helps shield portfolios from inflationary risks.

Scarcity and Industrial Demand

Platinum plays a crucial role in sectors such as green energy, automotive, and healthcare, while being mined in limited geographies.
