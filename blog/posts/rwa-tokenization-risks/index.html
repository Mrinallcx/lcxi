What Risks Are Associated With RWA Tokenization?

June 20, 2025 - 5 min read

Tokenization of real-world assets (RWA) has emerged as a powerful means of bringing tangible assets onto blockchain networks. Tiamonds is leading this innovation by offering tokenized access to four premium asset classes: diamonds, gold, silver, platinum and sapphires. Each token is backed 1:1 by a real, authenticated asset stored securely in physical custody. While this model offers transparency, accessibility, and blockchain-based security, several risks must be addressed to ensure safe adoption. This blog outlines these risks and presents how Tiamonds manages them through regulation, security, and trust-first infrastructure.

Key Highlights

  • Tokenizing RWAs introduces legal, pricing, and custody complexities.
  • Cybersecurity risks and limited liquidity remain key concerns in adoption.
  • Tiamonds manages these risks through MiCA compliance, certified asset validation, and secure infrastructure.
  • Understanding these challenges is vital for safe participation in the tokenized RWA space.

1. Why It’s Important to Understand the Risks

Tokenization of real-world assets is not just a technological advancement; it is a shift in how ownership and value transfer are perceived. Assets such as diamonds, gold, silver, platinum, and sapphires can now be traded on-chain through tokens backed by their physical counterparts. This bridge between traditional commodities and blockchain enables faster, more transparent, and borderless transactions of assets.

Understanding the risks involved is crucial to navigating this new environment with confidence. From regulatory challenges to cybersecurity vulnerabilities, each risk area impacts trust, adoption, and user protection. The sections below break down these risks in detail and explain how users can engage securely through thoughtful design and risk mitigation strategies offered by platforms like Tiamonds.

2. Legal and Regulatory Risks

One of the most significant challenges in RWA tokenization is the legal framework that governs digital ownership. These frameworks are often inconsistent or underdeveloped across jurisdictions, creating uncertainty for token issuers and buyers.

Key Concerns

  • Varying legal definitions of digital assets across countries
  • Ambiguity around investor rights and dispute resolution
  • Lack of global consistency in compliance and asset recognition

Managing the Risk

Tiamonds is fully regulated under Liechtenstein’s Financial Market Authority (FMA) and aligns with the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation. The platform ensures that each asset is tokenized within a compliant legal structure. Ownership rights, redemption conditions, and dispute policies are documented to provide legal clarity and investor confidence.

3. Valuation and Pricing Challenges

Real-world assets such as diamonds and precious metals often require expert appraisal. Market prices can vary based on certifications, quality grades, and market availability, making accurate valuation essential.

Key Concerns

  • Inconsistent pricing data from physical markets
  • Subjectivity in asset grading and appraisal
  • Discrepancies between token value and real-world asset value

Managing the Risk

Every Tiamonds asset is evaluated by certified experts and accompanied by third-party validation. Diamonds receive gemological certifications, while gold, silver, and sapphires are graded and documented before being tokenized. These reports are linked transparently to the token, allowing users to verify the value of the asset they hold.

4. Custody and Ownership Risks

For tokenized assets to retain value, the connection between the digital token and the physical item must remain secure and tamper-proof. Any weakness in this chain can compromise user trust.

Key Concerns

  • Loss or mishandling of physical items
  • Inaccessible or unverified storage
  • Unclear asset recovery or redemption rights

Managing the Risk

Tiamonds ensures that each asset is held in secure, insured, institutional-grade vaults. The custody process is fully documented, and every token issued is directly tied to a unique, physically stored asset. Clear redemption policies are provided, allowing token holders to claim their assets when necessary, subject to verification and compliance checks.

5. Technology and Cybersecurity Concerns

Blockchain-based platforms must be resilient to code vulnerabilities, infrastructure failures, and external cyber threats. These risks become especially critical when valuable real-world assets are involved.

Key Concerns

  • Exploits in smart contracts or token issuance logic
  • Platform downtime or disruptions
  • Risks from phishing, wallet hacks, or social engineering

Managing the Risk

Tiamonds employs regularly audited smart contracts and a hardened platform infrastructure to ensure user security and platform integrity. The security framework includes advanced encryption protocols, verified wallet connections, and multi-factor authentication. Users are guided through secure best practices to reduce the risk of scams and phishing attacks. Notably, our smart contracts have been audited by Hacken, a renowned blockchain security firm known for their rigorous and transparent audits.

6. Liquidity and Market Risks

Tokenized assets can face challenges in secondary market activity, especially if platforms do not support active trading or buyer interest remains low.

Key Concerns

  • Thin order books or limited market activity
  • Difficulty reselling tokens at fair market value
  • Delays in asset redemption or withdrawal

Managing the Risk

Tiamonds ensures the secure and transparent trading of its tokenized real-world assets, such as diamonds, gold, and sapphires, through its dedicated Tiamonds Marketplace. Here, each asset is represented as an NFT (ERC-721), allowing holders to list their assets for fixed-price sales or auctions, thereby fostering a decentralized and user-driven trading environment. Additionally, these NFTs are compatible with and tradable on external platforms that support the ERC-721 standard, expanding visibility and liquidity beyond the Tiamonds ecosystem. 

7. Final Thoughts

The rise of RWA tokenization has opened new avenues for bringing real-world value onto the blockchain. However, navigating this space requires awareness of the underlying risks and confidence in the platform facilitating such transactions. Tiamonds addresses these challenges through certified asset onboarding, strong legal backing, audited smart contracts, and user-first security infrastructure. By managing the risks thoughtfully and transparently, Tiamonds is helping redefine ownership in a way that is both digital and deeply tangible.
